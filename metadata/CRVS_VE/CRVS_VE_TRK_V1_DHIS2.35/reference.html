VE - Stillbirth classification Stillbirth classification “Intrapartum stillbirth” is the death of a fetus who was alive at the onset of labour but who died before delivery. This can be determined by the presence of fetal heart sounds (fetal heart tones) on admission or prior to delivery, or by appearance of a "fresh" stillbirth (intact skin and fetus on delivery). "“Antepartum stillbirth” is the death of a fetus before the onset of labour. This can be determined by “macerated” appearance of the fetus upon delivery, in combination with absence of fetal heart sounds on admission. – Absence of fetal heart sounds on admission does not necessarily indicate an antepartum stillbirth, if the woman was admitted with labour already in progress.– Presence of fetal heart sounds on admission of a labouring woman does exclude the possibility of an antepartum stillbirth. “Stillbirth, unknown timing” should be circled if it is not possible to tell the time of death of the fetus default vjNZ3tj3ins
